Chicken Satay

Birria tacos have gained immense popularity in recent years for their irresistible combination of tender, juicy meat and rich, flavorful broth. This traditional Mexican dish features slow-cooked, seasoned meat, typically beef or goat, that is braised until it becomes fall-apart tender. The meat is marinated with a blend of aromatic spices such as dried chilies, cumin, garlic, and Mexican oregano, infusing it with layers of robust flavors. The cooking process involves slow simmering the meat in a fragrant broth until it reaches the perfect tenderness. The result is succulent and melt-in-your-mouth meat that is then shredded or chopped and served in warm tortillas.

Birria tacos are often accompanied by a side of consommé, the flavorful broth reserved from the cooking process, which serves as a dipping sauce to enhance the taco's taste. These tantalizing tacos are typically garnished with diced onions, chopped cilantro, and a squeeze of lime, adding a refreshing and zesty element to the rich and savory flavors. The combination of tender meat, aromatic spices, and the consommé dipping sauce make birria tacos a truly extraordinary culinary experience that will leave you craving for more.

Ingredients

For the birria:

  • 2 pounds beef chuck roast or goat meat
  • 4 dried guajillo chilies
  • 2 dried ancho chilies
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 small onion, roughly chopped
  • 1 tablespoon ground cumin
  • 1 tablespoon dried Mexican oregano
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 4 cups beef broth
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil

For Serving:

  • Corn tortillas
  • Diced onion
  • Chopped fresh cilantro
  • Lime wedges
  • Consommé (reserved broth from cooking)

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 325°F (160°C).
  2. Remove the stems and seeds from the dried guajillo and ancho chilies. In a dry skillet over medium heat, lightly toast the chilies for a minute or two on each side until fragrant. Transfer the toasted chilies to a bowl, cover them with hot water, and let them soak for about 15 minutes until softened.
  3. While the chilies are soaking, trim the excess fat from the beef chuck roast or goat meat, and cut it into large chunks.
  4. In a blender or food processor, combine the soaked chilies (drained), minced garlic, chopped onion, ground cumin, dried Mexican oregano, ground cinnamon, salt, pepper, and a splash of beef broth. Blend until you achieve a smooth and thick paste, adding more broth if needed.
  5. In a large oven-safe pot or Dutch oven, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add the meat chunks and sear them on all sides until browned.
  6. Pour the chili paste mixture over the seared meat and add the remaining beef broth. Stir to combine, ensuring the meat is well coated with the mixture.
  7. Cover the pot with a lid and transfer it to the preheated oven. Let the meat braise for about 3 to 4 hours, or until it becomes tender and easily falls apart when shredded with a fork.
  8. Once the meat is tender, remove it from the pot and shred it using two forks. Set aside.
  9. To prepare the tacos, warm the corn tortillas on a skillet or comal over medium heat until they are pliable and slightly toasted.
  10. Fill each tortilla with a generous portion of the shredded meat. Fold the tortilla in half or roll it into a taco shape.
  11. Serve the birria tacos with diced onion, chopped fresh cilantro, and a squeeze of lime juice. The tacos are traditionally accompanied by consommé, the flavorful broth reserved from cooking, for dipping.
