Chicken Satay

Chicken satay is a delectable Southeast Asian dish that combines tender pieces of marinated chicken with a rich and flavorful peanut sauce. This recipe is a tantalizing blend of sweet, spicy, and savory tastes that will transport your taste buds to the vibrant streets of Malaysia or Thailand.

To create this mouthwatering dish, chicken strips are marinated in a mixture of aromatic spices like turmeric, cumin, and coriander, along with garlic and ginger, lending a delightful depth of flavor. The marinated chicken is then skewered and grilled to perfection, resulting in succulent, charred edges that enhance the overall taste. Finally, the chicken satay is served with a velvety peanut sauce made from creamy peanut butter, coconut milk, soy sauce, lime juice, and a touch of chili for a slight kick. This dish is a crowd-pleaser, ideal for gatherings and as an appetizer, or it can be enjoyed as a main course with steamed rice or alongside a fresh cucumber salad. The combination of tender chicken, fragrant spices, and the rich peanut sauce make chicken satay an irresistible culinary experience that will leave you craving for more.

Ingredients

  • Chicken breast or boneless chicken thighs (about 1 pound)
  • Wooden skewers (soaked in water for at least 30 minutes)

    • For the marinade:

    • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
    • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
    • 2 tablespoons lime juice
    • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
    • 2 cloves garlic, minced
    • 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
    • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
    • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
    • 1 teaspoon ground paprika (optional for added color)
    • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil

    For the peanut sauce:

    • 1/2 cup creamy peanut butter
    • 1/4 cup coconut milk
    • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
    • 2 tablespoons lime juice
    • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
    • 1 tablespoon sriracha sauce (adjust to your spice preference)/li>
    • 1 clove garlic, minced
    • Water (as needed to adjust the consistency)

    Optional garnish:

    • Chopped peanuts
    • Fresh cilantro or parsley
    • Lime wedges

Steps

  1. In a bowl, combine all the marinade ingredients: soy sauce, fish sauce, lime juice, brown sugar, minced garlic, turmeric, cumin, coriander, paprika (optional), and vegetable oil. Mix well to form a smooth marinade.
  2. Cut the chicken breast or boneless chicken thighs into thin strips, about 1/4 to 1/2 inch thick.
  3. Place the chicken strips into the marinade, ensuring they are well coated. Cover the bowl and let the chicken marinate in the refrigerator for at least 1 hour, or ideally, overnight for maximum flavor.
  4. Meanwhile, prepare the peanut sauce. In a small saucepan over low heat, combine peanut butter, coconut milk, soy sauce, lime juice, brown sugar, sriracha sauce, and minced garlic. Stir well until the sauce is smooth and creamy. If needed, add a little water to adjust the consistency. Keep the sauce warm or reheat it before serving.
  5. Preheat your grill or grill pan over medium heat. If using wooden skewers, make sure they have been soaked in water for at least 30 minutes to prevent them from burning.
  6. Thread the marinated chicken strips onto the skewers, evenly distributing them. Leave a small gap between each piece to ensure even cooking.
  7. Place the skewers on the preheated grill and cook for about 3-4 minutes on each side, or until the chicken is cooked through and has a nice char on the edges. Make sure to rotate the skewers for even cooking.
  8. Once the chicken is cooked, remove the skewers from the grill and let them rest for a few minutes.
  9. Serve the chicken satay with the warm peanut sauce on the side. Garnish with chopped peanuts, fresh cilantro or parsley, and lime wedges, if desired.
